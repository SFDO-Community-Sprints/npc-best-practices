# Maximizing Partner Interactions

In the event you are going to evaluate partners to assist with the migration effort, how can you maximize your interactions with them?

* Personas impacted.

* How NPSP is being used today, where it is meeting your needs out of the box vs. areas of high customization (and the reasons why this was needed).

* Business processes that should be reconsidered, if any, as part of the migration.

* High priority business processes that need to be added, if any.

* Assessment of data - tables, custom fields, records counts, quality.

* Org assessment - automations and/or code by object.

* Adoption successes and/or challenges.

* Reporting overview in addition to what’s working well and gaps.

* Current integrations and satisfaction with them. High priority integrations that need to be added, if any.

* Security requirements.

As for things to think about when you’re evaluating what partner to use, think about the following:

* Have they worked in your sub-sector before? Every institution is different, but there’s often more overlap than you think with peer institutions.  A partner that’s worked in your space may be able to more easily “speak your language.”

* What do they want you to do? Partners are just that…partners!  They’ll work with you and bring their expertise to bear to implement AFNP for you, but partners generally won’t disappear for months and then reappear with your migrated org. You know how you operate better than they do, and they’ll need your expertise to be successful.  However, your work doesn’t stop because you’ve decided to implement AFNP. Make sure that your partner isn’t assuming you’ll be able to do things you can’t or don’t have time to do.

* How well do you work with them?  You’re going to be spending a lot of time with your partner’s project time. If you don’t vibe well, it could be a miserable experience for you and them.

* How do they operate?  Some partners operate on a Time & Materials basis (where you pay for their time and anything they use); others operate on a fixed fee basis (where you pay a flat fee for their deliverables); still others operate on a subscription or managed services basis (where you pay a recurring fee for their services). Each approach has pros and cons and the approach you end up with will depend on the partner, the complexity of the project, and how your organization operates. Some partners are flexible on how they structure their engagements and will work with you. Fixed fee engagements may be more cost-effective, but *only if your migration is well defined* (use the tools above to help). If the scope of the migration is poorly defined, you’ll see that fixed fee keep rising as more and more things get added.
